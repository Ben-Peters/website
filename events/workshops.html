Workshops

Join us as we invite guest speakers to lead a series of workshops on how to set up and run high quality sound and lighting events! They will include an engaging mixture of presentations, discussions, and hands-on experience.

These workshops are free and open to the WPI Community. Anyone who is interested can attend, regardless of skill level!

While we recommend you attend as many sessions as possible for the complete experience, you can feel free to pick which workshop sessions seem most interesting to you. Just know that information from previous workshop sessions may be assumed and thus will not be covered again.

How to Sound

Instructors: Andrew Flynn and Chris Pierce

Description: Learn everything from setting up your first PA to running sound events with hundreds of unique channels and processing elements. All skill levels encouraged. Introductory topics are covered on day one, and day two will contain more advanced subjects. The schedule is available here. Please come and go based on what you find interesting/helpful.

Location: HL116 (Higgins Labs)

Date: Sept. 28th from 10am to 5pm and Sept. 29th from 11am to 5pm

Introduction to Lighting

Instructor: Valerie Moore

Description: The lighting workshop is designed to teach students how conventional and intelligent fixtures, consoles, dimmers, and DMX all work together to illuminate anything from a single light to a full musical theater show. Students will learn everything from what lighting fixtures are to how to plan a show with 30+ lighting fixtures. During the practical portion (in Alden) students will get hands-on experience with the fixtures and consoles they learned about in the classroom.

In AK233 (Atwater Kent):

Sept. 8 [5:30-7pm]: Lighting Theory 101

Sept. 15 [5:30-7pm]: Colorblasts and Consoles

Sept. 22 [5:30-7pm]: Paperwork, Plots, and Planning

In Alden:

Oct. 6 [12-3pm]: Lighting Fixtures: What, Where, and How

Oct. 21: Intro to the Avo: Busking a Live Show*

Nov. 4: Advanced Avo: Making a Live Show Pop*

Nov. 10: Intro to the Gio: Programming for a Show*

Nov. 24: Advanced Gio: Programming the Cool Stuff*

*Requires registration. We will try to accommodate as many people as possible, but due to the hands-on nature of these sessions space will be limited. Preference will be given to those who have attended previous workshop sessions. Registration will open after the Sept. 22nd workshop.
